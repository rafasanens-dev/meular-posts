# Pattern Analysis: @tokyohousing.group

Profile: https://www.instagram.com/tokyohousing.group/
Analyzed: 2026-04-13
Sample size: 4 Reel posts (no carousels found)
Format: Reels only

**Note:** This profile publishes exclusively Reels. While it uses a different format than what @meular.jp will produce (carousels), it is the most relevant benchmark for Japan-based foreign-audience real estate content. Patterns extracted are for reference on caption format, spec presentation, and Japan-specific CTAs.

---

## 1. Caption Structure

**Template observed:**
```
Comment [NUMBER] for more info!
[TYPE]: [Beds/Size spec]
[Feature] / [Feature] / [Feature]
[Price or Rent]: ¥[amount]
[Notable feature: No key money / Parking / Pet allowed]
```

**Alternative opening (luxury/named building):**
```
For Rent: [Building Name]
[TYPE] | [sqm]
[Feature] / [Feature]
```

---

## 2. Content Patterns

- **"Comment [NUMBER]"** mechanic: each listing has a unique comment code. Followers comment the number, the account replies with full details or DM — this drives algorithmic engagement + captures leads in comments
- Specs are always inline in the caption (not on slides): bed count, sqm, station access, price
- Station proximity always mentioned: "[Station name] X minute walk" — critical differentiator for Japan market
- "No key money" explicitly called out — removes a common barrier for foreigners
- "Pet allowed" is a major highlight (most Japanese apartments prohibit pets)
- LINE is the primary contact channel (not WhatsApp, not DM)

---

## 3. Hooks & Value Propositions

- "Comment [NUMBER] for more info!" — creates engagement loop and lead signal
- "Newly built" / "Freehold" / "No key money" — removes friction and overcomes common foreign buyer objections
- "Pet allowed" + "Students friendly" — niche targeting signals
- LINE CTA with ¥10,000 off: "Add us on LINE to receive weekly handpicked listings and ¥10,000 off on your contract!" — strong incentivized opt-in

---

## 4. Tone & Language

- Simple, direct English — non-native friendly
- Technical specs delivered as slash-separated facts (no narrative)
- Minimal emojis; functional use only
- No hashtags on individual posts (observed), or very minimal
- Profile-level hashtags: #tokyorealestate #tokyolife #japanrealestate #realestatejapan #tokyohousing

---

## 5. Applicable Lessons for @meular.jp

- **Station/transport proximity** must always appear in listings — e.g., "5 minutos a pé da estação X"
- **"No key money" / "No gift money"** equivalents should be highlighted when applicable (大きな差別化要因)
- **Pet allowed** should be explicitly called out when true
- **LINE CTA** is less relevant for @meular.jp's primarily Brazilian audience (WhatsApp is the priority), but consider maintaining LINE as secondary contact for Japanese-speaking or other Asian expats
- **Comment-for-info mechanic** could be adapted: "Comente o número [X] para receber mais informações!" — boosts reach without extra effort
- **Spec density** approach (station + sqm + beds + price all inline) is appropriate for the Japanese market audience — Brazilians in Japan will expect this clarity
- **"Newly built" / "Just renovated"** highlights resonate — @meular.jp offers renovation packages, so this is especially relevant
- **Incentivized contact CTA**: adapt the ¥10,000 off mechanic → "Entre em contato pelo WhatsApp e ganhe uma consulta gratuita"
