## 1. Universal Caption Structure (Cross-Profile)

All high-performing real estate carousel posts follow a consistent 4-part anatomy:

```
[HOOK] — opens with story, headline, address, or code
[NARRATIVE] — lifestyle/property description (2-4 sentences)
[SPECS] — beds, baths, size, price (inline or block)
[CTA] — contact, visit, swipe, comment
```

The order of HOOK and SPECS varies by brand tier:
- **Premium/aspirational** (@theagencyre, @lopesimoveis): story first, specs last
- **Volume/transparency** (@serhant): address + price first, story second, specs repeated at end
- **Speed/efficiency** (@tokyohousing.group): specs first, story optional

**Recommendation for @meular.jp:** Use story-first for premium listings (emphasis on Japan experience, lifestyle, location) and address-first for mid-range listings (practical buyers who want quick info).

## 8. Japan-Specific Adaptations (Critical)

These are unique to the Japanese market and absent from the Western reference profiles:

1. **Always mention station + walking time** — this is the primary location signal in Japan
2. **Pet allowed must be explicit** — most rentals and many sales-targeted neighborhoods are strict
3. **Renovation status** — "Recém reformado" or "Com reforma inclusa" is a major differentiator (@meular.jp's unique selling point)
4. **Price in ¥** — do not convert to BRL; buyers in Japan think in yen
5. **WhatsApp as primary CTA** — not LINE (LINE is for Japanese/Asian expat audience; @meular.jp's base is Brazilian/Hispanic)
6. **Japanese bureaucracy reassurance** — "Te ajudamos com toda a documentação" reduces purchase anxiety for foreigners

5. **Japan market signals must be present**: station proximity, pet policy, renovation status
6. **Tone**: warm and direct Portuguese — not formal corporate, not overly casual; like a trusted advisor
